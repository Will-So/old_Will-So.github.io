Data Projects

Here are some interesting data projects that I have worked on.

Maximizing Returns in Peer to Peer Lending

Predicts the default rate of loans and automatically order the model's favorite currently available loans. Likely to increase ROI from 7.5% (Lending Club Average) to 13.5%. This project identifies investment stratagies that fit custom investment profiles. The user specifies the amount of capital she is willing to invest and either her desired expected return or her maximum risk tolerance.

Key Features:

  1. Choosing the model's favorite loans (top 20%) leads to an expected ROI of 13.5%
  2. Provides a web app to view the currently available loans with their predicted ROI and default probability.
  3. Use the lending club API to order the model's favorite loans automatically.

Making Sentiment Analysis better with Yelp

This is the first part of a larger project that will make more sophisticated restaurant recommendations based on the specific preferences of the user.

This part of the project investigates how well we can predict the rating of a review based on the text of the review. Interestingly, SVM performed much better than Random Forests in this problem. This is probably because of the larger feature space.

My model does an excellent job of identifying Negative (1, 2 stars) and positive reviews (4, 5 stars) yet struggles to correctly identify neutral reviews. It tends to identify most neutral reviews as being positive.

You can find the blog post here and the github repository here for more details.

The next step will be to make the recommendation system in all of its glory.

Which Films should have remakes or sequels?

Over the past couple of years companies like Netflix and Amazon has had much success producing TV series for their subscribers. What would this strategy look like in the case of movies? I use data publicly available from Box Office Mojo and IMDB to find the films that are most likely to have a successful sequel.

Here were the most important factors and their influence on ROI:

Factor Change in ROI
Released in Month of October \(\Uparrow 5\%\)
\(\Uparrow\) Budget 10% \(\Downarrow 5.2\%\)
\(\Uparrow\) Audience Rating 1% \(\Uparrow\) 6.3%

Given these factors, here is a list of movies whose sequels are most likely to have a higher ROI:

  1. Gone with the wind
  2. Juno
  3. Crocodile Dundee
  4. Blair Witch Project
  5. The Rocky Horror Picture show
  6. National Lamboon's Animal House
  7. Platoon
  8. Fahrenheit 9/11
  9. Magic Mike
  10. The Usual Suspects.
